REMOVE BEFORE FLIGHT

REMOVE BEFORE FLIGHT (RBF-1) is a 2000 lbf Liquid Oxygen, Liquid Natural Gas or Methane engine currently being designed and developed by the Rocket Propulsion Laboratory. A1â€™s first static fire is planned for August of 2017.

As with all RPL projects, RBF-1 is an opensource project. Click the link to source on under Technical Specifications to the right to view all of our technical document.

TECHNICAL OVERVIEW

RBF-1 is the first iteration of this engine design. The engine is designed to use LOX/Methane, a cheap propellant combination which can be synthesized on Mars using the Sebatier reaction.

Technical Specifications

Thrust: 1000 lbf

Propellants: LOX/LNG

Chamber Pressure: 300 psi

Specific Impulse: 288 s

Characteristic Velocity (c*): 5929 ft/s

Material: Inconel 718

Some Cool Things

Ignition

The vast majority of liquid-propellant engines developed at the collegiate level use small solid-propellant motors thrust up the chamber of the engine for ignition. While this method is simple, the flame can by extinguished before the propellants ignite. For RBF, we decided to use an Augmented Spark Igniter. This is the same type of igniter used in the SSME or RS-25. This method uses a small spark plug at the back of small igition tube to create a pilot flame which is subsequently used to ignite the main combustion chamber. This method is defintiely more challenging, but we are confident that the results will prove worth while.

Injection

Yet another area where we decided to pursue a more challenging option is in injection. Injection is one of the most important aspects of engine efficiency. The efficiency of an engine is very nearly directly proportional to the efficiency of injection. Two goals that we aimed for in our design were efficient mixing and throttleablility. In 2018-2019, RPL will begin working on the second revision of Remove Before Flight, RBF-2. RBF-2 will be a slightly larger engine with the ability to throttle and vector its thrust. By designing with this in mind, the current RPL members are preparing for success for years to come.
