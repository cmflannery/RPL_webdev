ARIEL-1

Ariel-1 (A1) is a 2000 lbf Liquid Oxygen, Liquid Natural Gas or Methane engine currently being designed and developed by the Rocket Propulsion Laboratory. A1â€™s first static fire is planned for August of 2017.

As with all RPL projects, Ariel-1 is an opensource project. Click the link to source on under Technical Specifications to the right to view all of our technical document.

TECHNICAL OVERVIEW

Ariel-1 is the first iteration of the Ariel engine. The engine is designed to use LOX/Methane, a cheap propellant combination which can be synthesized on Mars using the Sebatier reaction.
